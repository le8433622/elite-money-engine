# Go To Market

## Initial audience
- individuals who want better spending control
- freelancers who manage personal and work cash flow
- small operators who need simple financial visibility

## Launch approach
### Stage 1
Invite a small beta group and validate product usefulness.

### Stage 2
Open a landing page and a simple free plus paid plan structure.

### Stage 3
Use content, referrals, and direct outreach to grow adoption.

## Main acquisition channels
- content marketing
- personal network and founder-led outreach
- creator partnerships in personal finance and productivity
- referrals from early users

## Conversion priorities
- clear landing page
- fast onboarding
- strong dashboard first impression
- visible automation value
- simple upgrade path from Free to Pro

## Success signals
- users return weekly
- users create automation rules
- users enter repeat transactions
- some free users upgrade to paid plans
